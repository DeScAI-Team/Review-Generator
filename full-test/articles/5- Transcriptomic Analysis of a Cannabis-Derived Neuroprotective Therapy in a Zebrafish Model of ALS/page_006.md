**Product Efficacy Assessment**

BMAA treatment significantly reduced locomotion in zebrafish larvae (p < 0.0001), confirming the validity of the neurodegeneration model. Edaravone, a current ALS medication, demonstrated significant rescue effects (p = 0.0002) on BMAA-treated larvae mobility. Both CNR-401 and cannflavin A showed significant rescue effects with p-values of p < 0.0001 and p = 0.0054, respectively (Figure 1A). All treatments were administered at a 2 µM concentration. CNR-401 displayed dose-dependent efficacy with the lowest effective dose of 0.5 µM in the BMAA-induced neurotoxicity model (Figure 1B), exhibiting an estimated EC₅₀ of 0.0012 µM. Cannflavin A required a higher concentration, showing efficacy beginning at 1 µM with an estimated EC₅₀ of 0.004 µM (Figure 1C). This difference indicates superior potency for CNR-401 at lower concentrations.
