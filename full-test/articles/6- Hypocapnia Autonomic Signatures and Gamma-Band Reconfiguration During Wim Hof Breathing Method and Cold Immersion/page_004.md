Furthermore, meta-analytic evidence indicates that structured breathwork reduces perceived stress and improves stress-related (medium effect size), anxiety, and depressive symptoms in randomised studies, with small-to-moderate effects, and that slow breathing increases heart-rate-variability (HRV) indices of vagal control<sup>6–7</sup>. Together, these findings suggest that the WHM could produce robust systemic effects, but neural endpoints have been sparsely measured, and studies using concurrent EEG and electrodermal activity are lacking. Accordingly, our study set out to (i) quantify WHM’s neural and autonomic effects (EEG, EDA/GSR, capnography/SpO₂), (ii) compare naïve practitioners of the WHM with no prior or only minimal WHM exposure (operationally: 0 years of practice; see Table 1) with Level-2 (L2) experienced practitioners (operationally: ~6 years of practice, range 4–7 years).<sup>8</sup>
