When considering the Naive group, significant FC increases were found in the beta (13–30 Hz), mid-gamma (55–70 Hz), and high-gamma (70–95 Hz) ranges (Figure 12). In the beta band (Figure 12A), enhanced connectivity is linked to posterior midline regions—particularly the precuneus and isthmus cingulate—with lateral occipital and parahippocampal cortices, indicating stronger integration within posterior default-mode and visual-associative networks. In the mid-gamma range (Figure 12B), connectivity was strongest between the right superior and transverse temporal regions and the left occipital areas (lingual and lateral occipital cortices), with additional bilateral couplings among the parahippocampal, fusiform, entorhinal, and temporal cortices, reflecting the engagement of medial temporal and high-level visual systems. In the high-gamma band (Figure 12C), increased connectivity emerged between right superior and transverse temporal areas and bilateral occipitotemporal regions, including lingual, lateral occipital, and fusiform cortices. Additional links involving medial temporal, posterior cingulate, and midline sensorimotor regions (paracentral, cuneus) suggest integrated activation across perceptual, mnemonic, and sensorimotor networks.
