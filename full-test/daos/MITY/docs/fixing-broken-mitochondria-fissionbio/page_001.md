# Fixing Broken Mitochondria: A New Path to Brain Health

## Introducing FissionBio

Brain cells burn through energy at an extraordinary rate. Every thought, every memory, every movement depends on hundreds of mitochondria packed into each neuron.

But all that activity takes a toll. Mitochondria can become damaged, start fragmenting, and gradually lose their ability to power the cell. This isn't just a byproduct of aging – it appears to play an active role in diseases like Alzheimer's, ALS, and Huntington's.

These diseases have long been treated separately, each with its own mechanisms and drug programs. But researchers have uncovered a common thread – an interaction between the proteins Drp1 and Fis1 causing mitochondria to fragment excessively.

FissionBio, backed by community funding through Cerebrum DAO, is developing drugs targeting this interaction. They're exploring a fundamental mechanism of neurodegeneration – how mitochondrial imbalance can push neurons toward failure – and designing small molecules to stop it at the source.

Read on to see how broken mitochondria became the focus – and how decentralized funding is pushing their research forward.

## Understanding the Link Between Mitochondria and Disease

Every cell in your body contains hundreds or thousands of mitochondria, but nowhere are they more critical than in your brain.

Neurons are energy-hungry cells that never stop working, requiring a constant supply of ATP (cellular fuel) to maintain electrical activity, transport materials, and repair themselves.

In healthy cells, mitochondria maintain a delicate balance between two opposing processes: fusion and fission. This dynamic system allows mitochondria to adapt while maintaining optimal function.
