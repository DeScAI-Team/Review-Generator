- **Risk control:** Willingness to fund innovative but unproven concepts that might be too early for traditional investors  
- **Transparent funding:** Open processes that communities can track and evaluate, ensuring accountability  
- **Patient engagement:** Direct involvement of people affected by these diseases in funding decisions  

By pooling resources and expertise, BioDAOs bridge the gap between academic research and commercial development. With community backing, promising science can move forward based on its potential to help people rather than its appeal to profit-focused investors.

## What Comes Next

FissionBio has demonstrated that its scientific concept is effective in the laboratory. Now comes the process of turning promising compounds into medicines that can help patients.

### From Discovery to Medicine

Moving from laboratory success to actual medicines requires following a systematic drug discovery process. While SC9 demonstrated that small molecules can target the SWAG binding site and mimic P110's effects, it lacks the brain penetration needed for neurological applications.

FissionBio is now developing third-generation compounds that build on this knowledge while achieving better drug properties, particularly brain penetration. Starting with the validated SWAG target and binding site, the team must develop entirely new molecules that can effectively treat brain diseases.

The development process involves multiple stages:

- **Hit identification** – Screening for new compounds that bind to the SWAG site and show activity  
- **Lead optimization** – Refining molecular structures for better drug properties, especially brain penetration  
- **Safety testing** – Confirming compounds are non-toxic at therapeutic doses  
- **Pharmacokinetics** – Ensuring drugs reach their targets and clear safely from the body  
- **Disease model validation** – Demonstrating efficacy in relevant animal models
