This selectivity is crucial because mitochondria require normal fission processes for quality control, mitophagy, biogenesis, and energy regulation. Since P110 only blocks the pathological Drp1–Fis1 interaction, which doesn't occur during healthy cellular processes, it preserves normal mitochondrial functions.

Over 13 years of research across 11 different disease models has built an impressive body of evidence for P110's therapeutic potential, showing remarkable benefits across multiple conditions:

*   **Huntington's disease models:** Reduced brain cell death and improved motor function, with researchers noting that "novel Drp1 inhibitor diminishes aberrant mitochondrial fission and neurotoxicity" (Qi et al., 2012).
*   **ALS patient cells:** Restored mitochondrial function and cellular viability in human neurons derived from patient models (Joshi et al., 2018).
*   **Alzheimer's disease models:** Enhanced cognitive performance and reduced neurodegeneration, with studies demonstrating that "Drp1/Fis1 interaction mediates mitochondrial dysfunction, bioenergetic failure and cognitive decline in Alzheimer's disease" (Joshi et al., 2018).
*   **Cardiac conditions:** Improved function in ischemic heart disease, such as pulmonary hypertension, showing that mitochondrial protection extends beyond the brain (Tian et al., 2017).
