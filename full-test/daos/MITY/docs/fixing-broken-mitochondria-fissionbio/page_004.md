# The P110 Breakthrough

What if you could block this destructive interaction without interfering with normal mitochondrial function?

That's exactly what P110 seeks to achieve – a peptide specifically designed to disrupt the Drp1-Fis1 binding while preserving essential cellular processes.

P110 represents a fundamentally new therapeutic strategy. Rather than trying to boost energy production or remove toxic proteins – common strategies in neurodegeneration research – P110 prevents the initial mitochondrial damage that triggers cellular dysfunction.

The peptide binds to the same site on Drp1 that pathologically interacts with Fis1. By occupying this binding site, P110 prevents inflammatory stress-induced fission while allowing normal mitochondrial dynamics to continue through other Drp1 receptors (Mff, MiD49, MiD51).
