# Fission Bio Project Announcement

## Fission Bio: Targeting Mitochondrial Dysfunction to Treat Neurodegeneration and Chronic Inflammation

Fission Bio is developing a novel class of small molecule inhibitors that selectively block pathological mitochondrial fission, addressing a fundamental mechanism linking chronic inflammation to neurodegeneration and age-related disease.

### The Core Idea

Mitochondrial dysfunction and fragmentation play central roles in neurodegenerative diseases and chronic inflammatory conditions. Most current approaches either target downstream symptoms or broadly suppress immune function. Fission Bio takes a different path by preserving mitochondrial integrity at its source, preventing the cascade of cellular damage without compromising normal immune signaling. The platform encompasses brain-penetrant, peripheral, and tissue-specific compounds, enabling broad clinical and commercial applications across multiple disease areas.

### The Science

The lead compounds inhibit the pathological interaction between dynamin-related protein 1 (DRP1) and mitochondrial receptor FIS1. Under normal conditions, mitochondria undergo controlled fission and fusion to maintain cellular energy balance. In disease states, excessive DRP1-FIS1 interaction drives mitochondrial fragmentation, leading to increased reactive oxygen species (ROS) production, bioenergetic failure, and downstream cell death and inflammatory cascades.

By selectively blocking this protein-protein interaction at the SWAG (switch I-adjacent groove) binding site on DRP1, Fission Bio's compounds preserve mitochondrial function and interrupt disease progression without inhibiting normal immune responses. This represents a fundamentally different approach compared to NLRP3 inflammasome inhibitors and other anti-inflammatory strategies that carry long-term immunosuppression risks.

The program originated from the P110 peptide platform developed at Stanford University, which has demonstrated efficacy across 11 disease models. Fission Bio is now translating this validated biology into drug-like small molecules through structure-based design.

### What Has Been Demonstrated

The scientific foundation rests on over 20 peer-reviewed publications establishing DRP1-FIS1 as a therapeutic target:

*   P110, the parent peptide compound, has shown efficacy in validated mouse models of ALS, Huntington's disease, Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, stroke, and cardiac ischemia
*   Patient-derived iPSCs from neurodegenerative conditions exhibit mitochondrial dysfunction that is reversed by P110 treatment
*   Published studies demonstrate that inhibiting mitochondrial fragmentation via P110 reduces neurodegeneration, slows ALS progression, and reverses bioenergetic failure and cognitive decline

Current screening progress includes:

**Current Status**

The team, led by Principal Investigator Dr. Luis Rios with support from translational neuroscience advisor Dr. Peter Groenen and project management by Dr. Maryna Polyakova, has completed hit confirmation and entered early optimization. The program operates through partnerships with Pharmaron as the CRO partner and is sponsored by Cerebrum DAO, which serves as IP custodian.

An IP-NFT has been minted, and composition of matter patents are planned for novel DRP1-FIS1 inhibitor scaffolds following hit-to-lead optimization. The IP strategy will cover three distinct classes: brain-penetrant inhibitors for CNS indications, peripheral inhibitors for systemic applications, and tissue-restricted compounds for localized use in organs such as the eye and gut. This clean freedom-to-operate pathway is built on de novo chemical scaffolds with no background IP restrictions.

**Next Steps**

With $200,000 in initial funding allocated by Cerebrum DAO ($84,508 disbursed to date) and additional community funding anticipated through an IPT round targeting approximately $500,000, the program is advancing through critical development milestones:

1. Completion of new compound synthesis and secondary screening by November 2025, with 300 additional compounds undergoing evaluation
2. Hit-to-lead optimization and IP filings for novel scaffolds in Q1 2026, selecting three lead scaffolds for advancement
3. In vivo validation in ALS, Huntington's disease, and Alzheimer's disease iPSC-derived models by mid-2026

The initial regulatory strategy targets ALS as the lead indication, enabling potential orphan drug designation, with plans to follow the standard FDA IND pathway after completion of lead optimization, in vivo efficacy studies, and safety profiling.

**The Opportunity**

ALS affects over 200,000 people worldwide, while the broader neurodegenerative disease market exceeds $200 billion globally. Additional addressable markets include Alzheimer's, Huntington's, Parkinson's, and ischemic heart disease, with potential expansion into age-related inflammatory conditions impacting hundreds of millions of patients.
