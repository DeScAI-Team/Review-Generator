# Percepta Project Announcement

## Percepta: Decentralized Brain Health Monitoring Through Digital Biomarkers and Blood-Based Testing

Percepta is developing a non-invasive, at-home cognitive monitoring platform that combines digital biomarkers with blood-based assays to detect early changes in brain health before clinical symptoms emerge.

### The Core Idea

Most cognitive decline goes undetected until significant symptoms appear, when intervention options are limited. Percepta addresses this gap by making sophisticated brain health monitoring accessible outside traditional clinical settings. The platform integrates remote blood collection with validated neurodegenerative biomarkers to enable longitudinal tracking of cognitive health, creating a scalable pathway for decentralized clinical assessments.

### The Science

The platform centers on phosphorylated tau protein (pTau-217), a validated biomarker strongly associated with Alzheimer's disease pathology. Through remote blood collection paired with digital cognitive assessments, Percepta identifies early-stage neurodegenerative signatures that serve as proxies for tau pathology and structural brain changes.

The approach leverages a proprietary formulation combining PTI-00703 Cat's claw extract and MemorTea oolong tea extract, protected under the Cognitive Clarity patent portfolio. This represents the first crowdsale-backed tokenization of a brain health patent portfolio through an IP-NFT structure, pioneering a new model for decentralized science (DeSci) funding.

### What Has Been Demonstrated

Preclinical validation shows compelling results:

*   In vitro assays demonstrate approximately 69.6% reduction in amyloid-beta fibril formation and 85.7% reduction in tau PHF/SF aggregation, outperforming commercial supplement comparators
*   Rodent studies using the Uncaria tomentosa-derived active ingredient show reduced amyloid plaque load, decreased neuroinflammation, and improved memory performance in validated animal models
*   These findings establish target engagement and provide mechanistic support for the clinical hypothesis
The scientific foundation draws on published literature demonstrating that polyphenols and proanthocyanidins from Uncaria tomentosa and oolong tea can reduce amyloid burden, modulate neuroinflammation, and support memory function.

**Next Steps**

With over $418,000 in committed funding across multiple initiatives, the program has reached critical development milestones:

1.  IRB final approval expected September 15, 2025
2.  Subject recruitment beginning September 16, 2025
3.  First data collection scheduled for November 1, 2025

The clinical study is designed as a non-therapeutic, structure-function trial evaluating biomarker and cognitive function changes. While the IRB has flagged potential IND requirements, the team has submitted a rebuttal emphasizing the study's non-drug classification. Future plans may explore the FDA botanical drug pathway contingent on clinical efficacy data.

**The Opportunity**

Over 10 million individuals in the United States experience early cognitive decline or subjective cognitive concerns, with potential global reach exceeding 50 million people. The global brain health supplements market was valued at $8.6 billion in 2022 and is projected to grow at 13.3% annually through 2030.

Percepta targets a critical market gap: the absence of human-evidence-backed memory supplements. While competitors like Prevagen report over $200 million in annual sales, they lack robust scientific validation. Percepta aims to establish category leadership through peer-reviewed preclinical data and a decentralized human study powered by tokenized funding.

Beyond the immediate supplement application, the Percepta model integrates proprietary IP, decentralized clinical research, and digital biomarker endpoints, creating a replicable blueprint for future DeSci-enabled studies across wellness and neurodegeneration pipelines. The tokenized IP-NFT and community-backed funding mechanisms represent an innovative approach to supporting brain health research.
