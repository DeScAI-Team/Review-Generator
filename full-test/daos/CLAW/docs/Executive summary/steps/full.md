# IRB protocol submitted to the ethics committee

## Executive summary

Percepta® for Cognitive Optimization: A 6-Month Randomized Controlled Trial with Neurocognitive, Wearable, and Participant-Reported Outcomes in Adults with Mild Cognitive Impairment

## Project Overview

This document details a protocol for a 6-month randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial to evaluate the efficacy and safety of Percepta, a plant-based dietary supplement containing cat's claw and oolong tea extract, in adults with self-reported Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI). The study, sponsored by Cerebrum DAO and conducted by Reputable Health, aims to determine if Percepta can improve cognitive performance, wearable biomarker metrics, and participant-reported brain health.

## Key Details

**Project Goal:** Conduct a decentralized clinical trial for safety and efficacy of Percepta in adults with self-reported Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI).
**Technology:** Percepta, a plant-based dietary supplement containing cat's claw and oolong tea extract.

# Milestone 1 Overview

## Background

MCI represents a critical stage between normal aging and dementia, with limited effective interventions. Percepta offers a novel, multi-faceted approach by leveraging the neuroprotective properties of its ingredients, which have shown promise in preclinical studies for reducing neuroinflammation, oxidative stress, and amyloid-beta plaque aggregation. Existing treatments for MCI, such as lecanemab and donanemab, have limitations in accessibility, cost, and safety, making alternative interventions necessary.

## Study Design

One hundred fifty-four participants aged 40 to 85 with self-reported MCI will be recruited and randomized 1:1 into either the Percepta or placebo group. The study will be conducted remotely, with participants receiving supplements at home and completing assessments via a mobile application and wearable devices (Oura Ring). Cognitive performance will be measured using the digital Montreal Cognitive Assessment (MoCA), wearable biomarker metrics (sleep quality, heart rate variability), and the Brain Health Index (BHI). An optional sub-study may assess blood biomarkers (tau protein and amyloid beta ratios). Interim analyses will be conducted at 3 months, with potential extensions to 9 and 12 months based on initial findings.

## Intervention

The active intervention is 750mg (2 capsules) of Percepta taken orally once daily. The placebo group will receive identical-looking capsules without active ingredients. Adherence will be tracked via a mobile application and wearable device data. Participants must maintain at least 80% adherence for per-protocol analysis and 95% for intention-to-treat analysis.

## Objectives and Endpoints

The primary objective is to determine if Percepta supplementation leads to significant improvements in cognitive performance as measured by the digital MoCA. Secondary objectives include improvements in wearable device biomarker metrics and BHI scores. Exploratory objectives may involve assessing blood biomarkers. Endpoints will be assessed at baseline, 3 months, and 6 months, with potential extensions to 9 and 12 months.

# Study Population

Inclusion criteria include adults aged 40 to 85, self-reported MCI (MoCA <25), ownership of an Oura Ring, and commitment to study procedures. Exclusion criteria include use of cognitive-affected medications, neurological or psychiatric disorders, allergies to ingredients, pregnancy, and APOE-4 allele carrier status. Participants must maintain stable lifestyles (diet, alcohol, substance use, physical activity, sleep) during the study.

# Safety and Monitoring

Safety will be monitored through continuous wearable device data, eligibility screening, and adverse event (AE) reporting. Participants will report AEs via a mobile application or email, and all AEs will be assessed for severity and relationship to the intervention. Serious adverse events (SAEs) will be reported to the IRB and sponsor within 48 hours. Participants will be provided compensation for completing the study.

# Scientific Rationale

The rationale for Percepta is based on the neuroprotective properties of cat's claw and oolong tea extract, which have shown potential in preclinical studies to mitigate neuroinflammation, oxidative stress, and protein aggregation. The randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled design ensures a rigorous evaluation of Percepta's efficacy and safety.

# Expected Outcomes

This study aims to provide foundational data on Percepta's safety, tolerability, and potential cognitive benefits. If successful, findings could inform future research and contribute to the development of accessible, natural interventions for individuals experiencing cognitive decline.
