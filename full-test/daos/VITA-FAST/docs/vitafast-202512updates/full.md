# MANDATORY INFORMATION

## Project Overview & Scientific Foundation

**Project Title:** VitaFAST
Brief, descriptive name for the therapeutic program

**Therapeutic Concept Summary:** Discovery and development of small-molecule activators of autophagy that rescue lysosomal dysfunction and cellular homeostasis. Compounds are identified via high-throughput phenotypic screening in autophagy-deficient cell models and optimized as first-in-class autophagy activators for aging and lysosomal storage disorders.

**Target Indication:** Longevity, Niemann-Pick type C (NPC), and other lysosomal storage disorders

**Patient Population Size:** Estimated number of patients who could benefit from the therapy

The VitaFAST program addresses multiple patient populations across its target indications:

*   **Niemann-Pick type C (NPC):** Ultra-rare genetic disorder with an estimated prevalence of ~1 per 100,000-120,000 live births globally. Diagnosed prevalent cases estimated at ~787 patients in the 7 major markets (2022), with ~335 in the US alone. Total NPC therapeutics market valued at ~$110-140 million (2024), projected to reach ~$210-260 million by 2032.
*   **Lysosomal storage disorders (broader category):** Estimated frequency of ~1 in 5,000 live births collectively. Global LSD therapeutics market valued at ~$9-11 billion (2025), growing at 4.5-8.4% CAGR to reach ~$13-20 billion by 2032-2034.
*   **Longevity/aging population:** Universal applicability – the entire aging population experiences autophagy decline. Global longevity and anti-aging drugs market estimated at $8.9 billion (2023), projected to reach $24.5 billion by 2032 (11.8% CAGR).
*   **Broader neurodegeneration:** Alzheimer's disease (50+ million patients globally), Parkinson's disease (10+ million globally) – conditions where autophagy dysfunction plays a key pathological role.

## Experimental Evidence

### Proof-of-Concept Data:
Experimental results supporting the mechanism with appropriate controls

*   Autophagy-deficient cells (Npc1-/-, Atg5-/-, Atg7-/-, FIP200/Rb1cc1-/-) show conserved cell-death phenotype under galactose stress; rescue is achievable by autophagy induction or downstream interventions (e.g., NAD+ modulation), establishing the assay biology.
*   Pilot screen of FDA-approved drugs identified 12 previously unknown autophagy inducers that activate autophagy flux in luciferase-p62 and LC3 “traffic-light” assays, validating the screening platform.
*   Thousands of compounds from natural and commercial libraries have been screened; multiple hit series with autophagy-activating signatures have been identified and advanced to derivative synthesis.

### Disease Model Information:
Specific models used and their validation status

*   Mouse embryonic fibroblasts (MEFs) with Npc1 knockout (Npc1-/-) modelling lysosomal dysfunction associated with NPC and Parkinson’s disease.
*   Autophagy-initiation knockouts (Atg5-/-, Atg7-/-, FIP200/Rb1cc1-/-) as negative controls to distinguish true autophagy-dependent rescue from non-specific survival effects.

### Dose-Response Relationships:
Quantitative potency data with IC50/EC50/ED50 values

Quantitative dose-response characterization ongoing. Key findings to date:

*   Third-round NCEs show 10-100x higher potency than earlier lead series
*   Commercially available compounds binding newly identified targets show highest potency in autophagy assays
*   Formal EC50/IC50 determination planned as part of lead optimization
*   Potency benchmarking against rapamycin and other commercial autophagy inducers completed

**Key competitors in autophagy modulation:**

*   **Rapamycin/Rapalogs (sirolimus, everolimus, temsirolimus):** FDA-approved mTOR inhibitors; well-established mechanism but significant side effects (immunosuppression); work by inhibiting mTOR upstream of autophagy
*   **Miglustat (Zavesca/Brazaves):** Approved for NPC in EU/Japan (not US); substrate reduction therapy; limited efficacy in clinical use
*   **Cyclodextrins (Trappsol Cyclo, Kleptose HPB):** Compassionate use for NPC; target cholesterol clearance rather than autophagy directly
*   **Metformin:** AMPK activator; autophagy-inducing effects secondary to metabolic mechanism

**VitaFAST differentiation:**

*   **Novel mechanism:** Direct autophagy activation via newly identified targets (distinct from mTOR inhibition)
*   **Lysosomal-specific rescue:** Compounds rescue lysosomal dysfunction specifically, not just autophagy initiation
*   **High potency:** Lead compounds show 10-100x higher potency than earlier series
*   **NAD preservation:** Mechanism directly addresses autophagy-NAD axis identified by Korolchuk lab
*   **Dual development paths:** Novel NCEs and drug repurposing opportunities
*   **First-in-class potential:** No approved drugs specifically targeting autophagy activation for longevity

## Strategic Context

Platform Potential:
Additional indications or broader technology applications

The VitaFAST autophagy activation platform has broad applicability:

* Neurodegeneration: Alzheimer's disease (protein aggregate clearance), Parkinson's disease (mitochondrial quality control), Huntington's disease
* Other lysosomal storage disorders: Beyond NPC – Gaucher disease, Fabry disease, mucopolysaccharidoses
* Metabolic diseases: Conditions where autophagy decline contributes to pathology
* Aging/longevity: Systemic cellular health maintenance
* Cancer: Context-dependent application (autophagy-dependent cell death in some cancers)
* Combination approaches: Potential synergy with NAD+ precursors (NMN, NR), sirtuin activators, or other geroprotectors
