# OPTIONAL BUT STRONGLY RECOMMENDED

## Experimental Evidence

### Proof-of-Concept Data:
Experimental results supporting the mechanism with appropriate controls

*   Autophagy-deficient cells (Npc1-/-, Atg5-/-, Atg7-/-, FIP200/Rb1cc1-/-) show conserved cell-death phenotype under galactose stress; rescue is achievable by autophagy induction or downstream interventions (e.g., NAD+ modulation), establishing the assay biology.
*   Pilot screen of FDA-approved drugs identified 12 previously unknown autophagy inducers that activate autophagy flux in luciferase-p62 and LC3 “traffic-light” assays, validating the screening platform.
*   Thousands of compounds from natural and commercial libraries have been screened; multiple hit series with autophagy-activating signatures have been identified and advanced to derivative synthesis.

### Disease Model Information:
Specific models used and their validation status

*   Mouse embryonic fibroblasts (MEFs) with Npc1 knockout (Npc1-/-) modelling lysosomal dysfunction associated with NPC and Parkinson’s disease.
*   Autophagy-initiation knockouts (Atg5-/-, Atg7-/-, FIP200/Rb1cc1-/-) as negative controls to distinguish true autophagy-dependent rescue from non-specific survival effects.

### Dose-Response Relationships:
Quantitative potency data with IC50/EC50/ED50 values

Quantitative dose-response characterization ongoing. Key findings to date:

*   Third-round NCEs show 10-100x higher potency than earlier lead series
*   Commercially available compounds binding newly identified targets show highest potency in autophagy assays
*   Formal EC50/IC50 determination planned as part of lead optimization
*   Potency benchmarking against rapamycin and other commercial autophagy inducers completed
